# Policy Anchors

Use these anchors to keep the audit neutral and source-backed.

## Research Misconduct Boundary

- ORI definition: research misconduct means fabrication, falsification, or plagiarism in proposing, performing, reviewing, or reporting research. It does not include honest error or differences of opinion.
- Source: https://ori.hhs.gov/definition-research-misconduct
- Use this to avoid verdict language. This skill documents risks and evidence, not institutional findings.

## Image Integrity

- Nature Portfolio image integrity policy requires that image adjustments preserve the original data; inappropriate enhancement, obscuring, moving, removing, or introducing features is not acceptable.
- For life-science gels and blots, journals commonly require source or unprocessed images, clear disclosure of splicing/cropping, and preservation of original relationships.
- Source: https://www.nature.com/nature-portfolio/editorial-policies/image-integrity

## Reporting and Registration Anchors

- ARRIVE 2.0: animal studies should report design, sample size, inclusion/exclusion criteria, randomization, blinding, outcome measures, statistical methods, experimental animals, procedures, and results clearly enough for assessment and reproduction.
- Source: https://arriveguidelines.org/arrive-guidelines

- ICMJE: clinical trials should be registered before enrolment begins; data-sharing statements are expected for clinical trials.
- Clinical registration FAQ: https://www.icmje.org/about-icmje/faqs/clinical-trials-registration/
- Data sharing: https://www.icmje.org/recommendations/browse/publishing-and-editorial-issues/clinical-trial-registration.html

- MIFlowCyt: flow cytometry publications should include enough information about samples, reagents, instrumentation, data analysis, and gating to interpret and reproduce the study.
- Source: https://flowcytometry.medicine.uiowa.edu/resources/miflowcyt-publication-standard

- NCBI SRA: stores high-throughput sequencing raw data and alignment information to support reproducibility.
- Source: https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sra/docs/

## Image-Concern Prevalence Context

- Bik, Casadevall, and Fang reported problematic image duplication in a non-trivial fraction of screened biomedical papers. Use this as context for why image screening matters, not as a presumption about any manuscript.
- PubMed record: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/27273827/
