# Reporting Standards Quick Reference

Use this file when the task involves methods, compliance, or missing-materials review.

## Animal Research

Check for:

- Study design and experimental unit.
- Sample size calculation or rationale.
- Inclusion and exclusion criteria.
- Randomization method.
- Blinding during allocation, intervention, outcome assessment, and analysis.
- Outcome measures, including primary outcome.
- Statistical methods and assumptions.
- Species, strain, sex, age, weight, source, health status.
- Housing, husbandry, welfare monitoring, humane endpoints.
- Ethical approval and protocol identifier.

Classify missing reporting as R1 or R2 unless there is a direct contradiction.

## Clinical Research

Check for:

- Trial registration and whether registration was prospective.
- Protocol and statistical analysis plan.
- Primary and secondary outcomes, with timing.
- CONSORT-style participant flow and exclusions.
- IRB/ethics approval.
- Informed consent.
- Randomization, allocation concealment, blinding.
- Adverse events and harms.
- Data sharing statement and access conditions.

Do not infer misconduct from an absent public protocol alone; mark the evidence gap.

## Cell and Reagent Research

Check for:

- Cell-line source and authentication.
- STR profiling date and match where relevant.
- Mycoplasma testing date.
- Passage number or range.
- Antibody target, clone, RRID, catalog, lot/batch, dilution.
- Reagent catalog and batch numbers.
- Positive, negative, vehicle, isotype, or FMO controls as appropriate.

If a claim depends on a reagent but the reagent cannot be identified, mark the finding as a reporting or completeness concern.

## Flow Cytometry

Check for:

- FCS files or source gates if supplied.
- Sample preparation and staining protocol.
- Antibody panel, fluorochromes, clones, RRIDs, lots.
- Instrument model, lasers, filters, voltage/settings where relevant.
- Compensation method and matrix.
- Gating hierarchy and representative plots.
- FMO/isotype controls when relevant.
- Denominator for each percentage.
- Analysis software and version.

Treat static gate images without FCS/workspace support as limited evidence.

## Omics

Check for:

- Repository accession: GEO, SRA, ArrayExpress, PRIDE, ProteomeXchange, metabolomics repository, or field-specific equivalent.
- Sample metadata and group mapping.
- Raw data or raw counts.
- QC metrics and excluded samples.
- Batch information.
- Normalization method.
- Differential analysis code and package versions.
- Multiple-testing correction.
- Link between sample IDs in paper, source data, and repository.

Missing accession or sample metadata usually creates an R1 completeness gap.
