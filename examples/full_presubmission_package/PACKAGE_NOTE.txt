Example package: FULL PRE-SUBMISSION (teaching sample).

This is a teaching example, not a real audit and not a real manuscript. The images are
synthetic textures, not real microscopy or blots. The package shows a realistic folder
layout for a pre-submission self-audit:

  manuscript.pdf
  figures/                 presentation-layer figure panels
  raw_images/              raw acquisitions the figures were exported from
  figure_assembly/         a structured manifest declaring figure -> raw links
  source_data/             numeric summaries behind the figures
  protocols/               sample map and methods notes
  statistics_code/         analysis notes

What you should expect in the report:
- Verified traceability: each figure panel is declared to come from a raw acquisition,
  and the image screen confirms the panel matches that raw file. This appears as
  positive provenance, not a reuse concern.
- Completeness gaps (R1): some expected materials (for example supplementary files and
  ethics/IACUC records for animal work) are not included, so the audit cannot be called
  complete.
- An Audit Coverage section listing what ran and what did not.

This example is deliberately "clean but incomplete". A clean automated result here does
NOT mean the study is proven correct; it means nothing was flagged within the supplied
materials and the current detector scope.
